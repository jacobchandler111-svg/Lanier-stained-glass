ROCK SPRINGS CHURCH
Elberton, Georgia

Location

Elberton, GA

Year

2024

Scope

Full sanctuary restoration

THE BUILDING

Rock Springs Church sits on the site of the original founding of the congregation in Elberton, Georgia. The current sanctuary was built in the early 1900s, and its leaded stained glass windows have looked over the congregation for more than a hundred years.

By the time we were called in, more than a century of weather, settling, and original-construction lead fatigue had taken a visible toll. The leading was bowing in several panels, multiple solder joints had failed, and a handful of glass pieces had cracked through.

THE PROJECT

We started with a full survey — every panel photographed, every condition note written down. The congregation got a copy of the report before any work began, with a clear scope and timeline.

Panels were removed one at a time and transported in custom crates to our Buford studio. Each was cleaned, the failed leading was carefully cut out, original glass pieces were preserved (cracked pieces stabilized rather than replaced wherever possible), and the panels were re-leaded with came matched to the original profile. Soldering and re-cementing brought everything back to weather-tight condition.
