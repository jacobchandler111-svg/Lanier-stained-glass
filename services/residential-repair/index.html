RESIDENTIAL STAINED GLASS REPAIR
in Buford, GA

A baseball through the transom. A panel that's started to bow. A solder joint that finally let go. Most repairs are smaller than they look and rarely require replacing the whole window. We assess in-home, repair in our Buford studio, and reinstall — preserving as much of your original glass as possible.

WHAT'S INCLUDED

  • · In-home assessment and written estimate
  • · Cracked / broken pane replacement (matched glass)
  • · Solder joint repair
  • · Re-leading panels with sagging or fatigued lead
  • · Re-cementing and weather-sealing
  • · Insurance documentation if needed

OUR PROCESS

  1. Assessment. In-home visit, photographs, written estimate.
  2. Removal. Panel removed, opening boarded for weather.
  3. Repair. Done in our Buford studio with matched materials.
  4. Reinstallation. Set, sealed, and walked through with you.

FREQUENTLY ASKED

My panel has a crack — should I fix or replace it?
Almost always: fix. A single cracked piece can usually be replaced without disturbing the rest of the panel, preserving the original glass and design. Full replacement is a last resort, used only when the leading itself has failed across most of the panel.
Can you handle insurance claims for damaged stained glass?
Yes. We'll provide a detailed written estimate, photo documentation of damage, and a summary of repair work suitable for submission to your insurance carrier.
Are antique panels safe to handle? I'm worried about lead paint.
The lead came in stained glass is metal, not lead-based paint, but the surrounding window frame in older homes may have lead paint. We follow safe-handling practices and recommend testing the frame separately if you plan to do any sanding or refinishing.
Do you do in-home repairs, or do you remove the panel?
It depends on the damage. Small solder joint repairs can sometimes be done in place. Anything involving glass replacement or re-leading needs the panel in the studio. We board the opening so your home stays weather-tight while we work.
